Financial Modeling of Companies

We are going to make our first model of the beloved fast food chain, McDonalds. I Think fast food is a really cool one to start with since most, if not all of us, have been to these kinds of places many times or have even worked in a fast food resturaunt before. We are already familiar with the menu but now we are going to dive deep into how McDonalds operates, where they make their money, how they spend it and so on! Let's start with a fresh workbook and format it so it's ready for use. Begin with a basic format like the one below.

Make a new tab by clicking the plus sign at the bottom left corner of your workbook and set it up like so.



We are going to record their current price per share and how many outstanding shares there are. When we multiply these two value together we will have the companies market cap. Then we are going to look up their most up to date cash on hand and long-term debt.

You may be wondering what 'EV' is. EV stands for enterprise value. This is an important value to look at as it gives a better representation of what the business is worth. We get EV by taking the market cap, adding the debt to it, and subtracting their cash. Enterprise value is another way of saying, "If I was going to buy this business today how much would I really have to spend". This should make sense because if you buy a business you are also buying whatever debt they have. Which is something the owner of the business will eventually have to pay. You also inherit whatever cash the business has which means you can ignore that much of price of the business. If you bought a business for 100 dollars but they had 40 dollars in cash, well you only really spent 60 dollars at the end of the deal.

And finally Net Cash is obtained by subtracting debt from current cash on hand.
